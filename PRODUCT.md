# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Slopgate is used by AI-assisted software engineers and maintainers who need to understand, tune, and trust guardrails across Claude Code, Cursor, Codex CLI, OpenCode, and project-local linting. They are usually in an active debugging or policy-maintenance workflow, looking for enough evidence to decide whether a rule, hook, prompt, or platform adapter needs action.

## Product Purpose

Slopgate provides real-time guardrails where host platforms support them, plus batch code-quality linting and an analytics dashboard for trace review. Success means operators can see what happened, why it happened, which project or rule is responsible, and what safe next action improves enforcement without normalizing debt or overstating platform parity.

## Brand Personality

Precise, operational, and candid. The product should feel like a flight recorder for enforcement systems: dense when the data matters, calm under pressure, explicit about platform limitations, and opinionated about safe repair paths.

## Anti-references

Avoid SaaS marketing gloss, decorative dashboards that hide raw evidence, parity claims unsupported by platform docs, and playful agent metaphors that obscure enforcement risk. Do not make rule management feel like generic project management; it must remain grounded in hook behavior, violations, and trace evidence.

## Design Principles

- Evidence before action: every recommendation should point back to trace, rule, hook, path, or platform signals.
- Tune without hiding risk: surface rule posture, overrides, skip scopes, and advisory gaps as operational evidence, not harmless settings.
- Platform honesty: clearly distinguish full, partial, degraded, and advisory-only support.
- Dense but navigable: preserve analytical depth while grouping decisions around the operator’s next task.
- Feedback loops are first-class: flagged behavior, agent review prompts, and improvement recommendations should be easy to launch from the dashboard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Respect reduced-motion preferences, preserve keyboard access for filters, tabs, toggles, and drilldowns, and do not rely on color alone for decisions, severity, or platform status.
